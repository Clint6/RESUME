GREEK METHOLOGY

Poseidon king of Atlantis

Posiedon is the Greek god of the sea, earthquakes, storms, and horses. He is one of the Twelve Olympians, the major deities of the Greek pantheon. He is the son of the Titans Cronus and Rhea, and a brother of Zeus and Hades. Poseidon is known for his powerful and sometimes volatile nature, often depicted with a trident, which he uses to control the seas and cause earthquakes.
